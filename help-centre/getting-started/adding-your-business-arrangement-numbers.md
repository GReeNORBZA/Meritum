A Business Arrangement (BA) number represents your billing relationship with Alberta Health. It is tied to a specific payment method (cheque or electronic deposit) and pay-to address. Every claim batch Meritum submits on your behalf is filed under your BA number, so Alberta Health knows where to send payment and assessment results.

You add your BA number in Step 3 of the onboarding wizard, right after setting up your professional profile.

## Entering your BA number

1. Type your **BA number** into the field provided. This is the number Alberta Health assigned when you registered your billing arrangement. You can find it on previous Statements of Assessment from Alberta Health, or by contacting Alberta Health directly.
2. If you bill under the **Primary Care Panel and Continuity Model (PCPCM)**, the wizard asks whether you participate in PCPCM. Select **Yes**, and a second BA field appears. PCPCM physicians need two active BAs: one for PCPCM panel-based payments and one for regular fee-for-service (FFS) claims. Enter both numbers. If you are not a PCPCM physician, you only need a single BA.
3. Click **Continue**. Meritum validates the format of each BA number and saves it to your account.

For more on what BAs are, how they work, and when you might need two, see [Business arrangements in Alberta](/help-centre/billing-reference/business-arrangements-in-alberta). For details on the dual-BA model, see [PCPCM: Primary Care Panel and Continuity Model](/help-centre/billing-reference/pcpcm-primary-care-panel-and-continuity-model).

## The AHC11236 submitter authorization form

After you enter your BA, Meritum pre-fills an **AHC11236 Submitter Authorization** form. This is an Alberta Health form that authorizes Meritum (as your accredited electronic submitter) to submit claims on your behalf under that BA number.

1. Click **Download AHC11236** to get the pre-filled PDF. Your legal name, billing number, and BA number are already populated from your profile.
2. Print the form, sign it, and submit it to Alberta Health by mail or fax. The form itself includes the mailing address and fax number.

You need to submit one AHC11236 per BA number. If you have two BAs (PCPCM and FFS), the wizard generates two separate forms.

## What happens while you wait

Your BA status starts as **Pending** immediately after you complete this step. Alberta Health typically processes the AHC11236 within two to four weeks.

While your BA is in Pending status, you can still use Meritum to create claims, run them through the validation engine, and save them. The only thing you cannot do is submit a batch to Alberta Health; batch submission requires at least one BA in **Active** status.

Once Alberta Health processes your AHC11236 and confirms the link, update your BA status to Active in your account settings under **Business Arrangements**. After that, batch submission is fully available and you can start filing claims electronically.
