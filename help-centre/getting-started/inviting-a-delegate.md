A delegate is a non-physician user who performs billing tasks on your behalf. You control exactly what each delegate can see and do. To add one, you send an email invitation from your account settings; the recipient has 72 hours to accept before the link expires.

## Sending an invitation

1. Go to **Settings > Delegates** and click **Invite Delegate**.
2. Enter the delegate's **email address**. Meritum sends an invitation containing a one-time activation link that expires after 72 hours. If the link expires before the delegate accepts, you can resend it from the same page.
3. Choose a **permission preset** or configure permissions individually (see below).
4. Click **Send Invitation**. The delegate appears in your list with a status of "Pending" until they accept.

When the delegate clicks the activation link, they create their own Meritum account (or sign in to an existing one) and the linkage between your physician account and their delegate account becomes active immediately.

## Choosing permissions

Every delegate invitation includes a permission set that controls what the delegate can do under your account. You can pick one of three presets or build a custom configuration.

**Full Access** lets the delegate create, edit, and submit claims; manage patients; and view analytics and reports. **Billing Only** limits the delegate to claim entry and patient lookups; they cannot view reports or modify your practice settings. **View Only** restricts the delegate to read-only access across claims and patients, which is useful for auditors or administrative reviewers.

If none of the presets fit, select **Custom** and toggle individual permissions on or off. You can change a delegate's permissions at any time from the delegate management screen without sending a new invitation.

## What delegates cannot do

Regardless of the preset you choose, delegates cannot manage other delegates, manage your subscription, export data, or view audit logs. These restrictions are built into the platform and cannot be overridden through custom configuration.

## Multi-physician delegation

A single delegate can serve more than one physician. Each physician-delegate relationship carries its own independent permission set. For example, a billing clerk might have Full Access under one physician and Billing Only under another. The delegate switches between physician contexts explicitly from their dashboard, and each context switch is logged.

If you invite someone who already has a delegate account with another physician, they do not need to create a new account. Accepting your invitation adds your practice to their existing delegate dashboard.

For details on editing permissions and removing delegates after the initial invitation, see [Managing delegates](/help-centre/your-account/managing-delegates). For information on how delegate access interacts with physician data separation, see [Delegate access and data separation](/help-centre/security-compliance/delegate-access-and-data-separation).
