The Rejection Analysis dashboard shows you which refusal codes keep coming back, how much revenue they cost, and whether your corrections are actually reducing future rejections.

## Opening the dashboard

Navigate to **Analytics** and select the **Rejection Analysis** tab. The dashboard defaults to the current month. Use the period selector to view a custom range or compare periods side by side. You can filter by payer (Alberta Health Care Insurance Plan or Workers' Compensation Board), health service code (HSC), or specific explanatory code.

## What the dashboard shows

The dashboard has four main panels:

1. **Rejection rate trend**: a line chart tracking your refusal rate over time. A declining trend means your billing accuracy is improving. A flat or rising line means the same issues are recurring.
2. **Top rejection codes**: the explanatory codes that appear most often on your refused claims, ranked by frequency. Each code shows the total revenue lost to that rejection, so you can see which codes cost the most.
3. **Rejections by HSC code**: a breakdown showing which service codes generate the most refusals. This helps you identify whether certain procedures or visit types are consistently causing problems.
4. **Rejection resolution funnel**: tracks what happens to refused claims after you receive them. It shows how many were corrected and resubmitted, how many were written off, and how many are still unresolved.

## What to look for

Three patterns matter most:

**Recurring rejection codes.** If the same explanatory code appears across multiple assessments, the root cause has not been addressed. For example, if you keep seeing a "referral required" rejection on specialist consultations, you may need to update your workflow to include referrals before submission rather than fixing them after refusal. See [Reading rejection codes](/help-centre/after-submission/reading-rejection-codes) for help interpreting specific codes.

**High-value rejections.** The top rejection codes panel ranks by frequency, but the revenue impact column tells a different story. A code that appears three times but costs $1,200 in lost revenue may deserve more attention than a code that appears ten times but costs $150. Sort by revenue impact to prioritize corrections that recover the most money.

**Unresolved rejections.** The resolution funnel shows refused claims that have not been corrected or written off. These represent revenue sitting on the table. Claims approaching the 90-day Alberta Health Care Insurance Plan (AHCIP) submission deadline are flagged so you can act before the window closes. For step-by-step correction instructions, see [Correcting and resubmitting refused claims](/help-centre/after-submission/correcting-and-resubmitting-refused-claims).

## Corrective action effectiveness

Below the resolution funnel, the **Corrective Action Effectiveness** metric answers a specific question: after you identified and fixed a rejection pattern, did it actually stop happening? The metric compares rejection rates for a given code before and after you made changes. A declining rate confirms the fix is working. If the rate stays flat, the correction may not have addressed the real cause.

## Weekly rejection alert digest

If your most recent assessment includes new rejections, Meritum sends a rejection alert digest summarizing the codes, affected claims, and suggested next steps. The digest arrives by email and appears in your in-app notifications. It highlights any codes that are trending upward so you can catch problems early rather than discovering them at month-end.
