You can fix and resubmit most refused claims directly from the claim detail view. Meritum shows you exactly what went wrong and walks you through the correction.

## Finding refused claims

Open the **Claims** page and use the status filter to select **Refused**. The filtered list shows every claim that Alberta Health Care Insurance Plan (AHCIP) or Workers' Compensation Board (WCB) rejected in your most recent assessment. Claims approaching the 90-day submission deadline are flagged with an urgent indicator so you can prioritize them.

You can also reach refused claims from the dashboard summary panel, which shows a count of refused claims from your latest assessment results.

## Understanding the refusal reason

Open a refused claim to see its explanatory codes. Each code includes:

- The official AHCIP code
- A plain-language description of what caused the refusal
- Corrective guidance explaining what to change

A single claim can carry more than one explanatory code. You need to resolve all of them before resubmitting. For a full breakdown of code categories and their meanings, see [Reading rejection codes](/help-centre/after-submission/reading-rejection-codes).

## Correcting the claim

1. On the claim detail page, review each explanatory code and its corrective guidance.
2. Select **Edit Claim** to open the claim in draft mode. The fields that need attention are highlighted based on the refusal reason.
3. Make the necessary changes. Common corrections include adding a missing diagnostic code, updating a health service code (HSC) that was retired, attaching a referral for specialist consultations, or fixing a facility number.
4. Save the claim. Meritum runs its validation engine automatically when you save, checking the corrected claim against the same 19 pre-submission rules that run before every batch. If validation finds additional issues, you will see them before the claim is queued.

For some common refusals, the claim detail view offers a one-click corrective action; for example, you can add a referral or search for a replacement HSC code without leaving the page.

## Resubmitting the corrected claim

Once the claim passes validation, select **Queue for Submission**. The claim moves into the next Thursday AHCIP batch (or the next WCB batch, depending on the payer). You will see the claim status change from Draft to Validated, then to Queued.

After submission, assessment results typically arrive by Friday. The claim detail page shows the full resolution history, including the original refusal, your correction, and the resubmission outcome.

## The 90-day submission window

AHCIP claims must be submitted within 90 calendar days of the date of service. If a refused claim is getting close to this deadline, Meritum flags it with increasing urgency on both the Claims page and the dashboard. A claim that passes the 90-day mark cannot be resubmitted through the standard process; you would need to contact Alberta Health for a late submission request.

Check your refused claims promptly after each assessment to leave enough time for corrections.

## Writing off a claim

If you decide not to resubmit a refused claim, you can write it off instead. Open the claim and select **Write Off**. You will be asked to confirm and provide a reason. Written-off claims remain in your records for audit purposes but are excluded from resubmission queues and outstanding claim counts.

## Tracking resubmission history

Meritum records every action on a claim: the original submission, the refusal, your edits, and the resubmission. You can view this history on the claim detail page under **Claim History**. If a claim has been refused and resubmitted more than once, the full sequence of events is preserved. To review refusal trends across your practice, see [Tracking rejection patterns](/help-centre/after-submission/tracking-rejection-patterns).
