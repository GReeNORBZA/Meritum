After your claims are submitted, the payer reviews them and returns assessment results. This article explains when results arrive, where to find them in Meritum, and what each outcome means.

## When results arrive

For Alberta Health Care Insurance Plan (AHCIP) claims, results follow the weekly batch cycle. Claims submitted on Thursday are typically assessed by Friday. Meritum retrieves the assessment file from H-Link automatically and matches each result to your submitted claims. You receive a notification when your results are ready.

For Workers' Compensation Board (WCB) claims, results arrive in a return file from the myWCB portal after WCB processes your uploaded batch. You download that return file and upload it to Meritum through **Submissions > WCB Batches > Upload Return File**. Meritum then matches results to your original claims.

## Where to find results

Assessment results appear in two places:

1. **Dashboard**: the claims summary panel shows counts of paid, held, and refused claims from your most recent assessment.
2. **Claim detail view**: open any claim from the Claims page to see its current status, the outcome code, and a plain-language explanation of what happened.

## The three outcomes

**Paid**: the payer accepted the claim and payment has been issued (or will follow the standard payment schedule). The claim moves to Paid status and no further action is needed.

**Held**: the payer is reviewing the claim or needs additional information before making a decision. The hold reason code appears on the claim detail page. Held claims resolve on their own once the payer completes their review; you do not need to resubmit. If additional information is required, the hold reason will indicate what is needed.

**Refused**: the payer rejected the claim. One or more refusal codes appear on the claim detail page along with a plain-language description of each code. Common refusal reasons include missing data, governing rule violations (such as exceeding a visit limit or missing a referral), and patient eligibility issues. You can correct the claim and resubmit it, or write it off if you choose not to pursue it. See [Reading rejection codes](/help-centre/after-submission/reading-rejection-codes) for a breakdown of common codes and what they mean.

## Adjusted claims

Sometimes the payer accepts a claim but pays a different amount than you submitted. This happens when a governing rule reduces the fee, a modifier is disallowed, or a duplicate payment prevention rule applies. Adjusted claims show as Paid with a note showing both the submitted amount and the assessed amount. The difference is explained by the adjustment code on the claim detail page.

## What to do next

For paid and adjusted claims, no action is required. For held claims, monitor the claim detail page for updates. For refused claims, review the refusal codes, correct the issue, and resubmit. See [Correcting and resubmitting refused claims](/help-centre/after-submission/correcting-and-resubmitting-refused-claims) for step-by-step instructions.
