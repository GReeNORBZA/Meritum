Meritum offers three subscription options. All of them include the same features; the only difference is price and billing frequency.

## Subscription options

**Standard monthly:** $279.00 per month, billed monthly to your credit card.

**Standard annual:** $2,790.00 per year, billed as a single annual charge. This works out to $232.50 per month, a saving of roughly 17% compared to the monthly rate.

**Early bird monthly:** $199.00 per month, billed monthly. This rate is available to the first 100 physicians who sign up and is locked in for your first 12 months. After 12 months, your subscription moves to the standard monthly rate of $279.00 per month unless you switch to an annual plan before that date.

All prices are in Canadian dollars (CAD). Goods and Services Tax (GST) at 5% is added to every charge. For example, the standard monthly subscription appears on your credit card statement as $292.95 ($279.00 plus $13.95 GST). Meritum Health Technologies Inc. is a Canadian company registered for GST collection.

## What is included

Every subscription tier includes the same set of features with no add-ons or paywalls:

- Full platform access for one physician account
- Alberta Health Care Insurance Plan (AHCIP) claim submission through H-Link
- Workers' Compensation Board (WCB) claim submission through Electronic Injury Reporting
- The rules engine, which validates your claims against Schedule of Medical Benefits (SOMB) governing rules, modifier logic, and fee schedules before submission
- The advice engine, which suggests missing modifiers, flags common rejection patterns, and highlights billing opportunities based on your claim history
- Analytics dashboards covering revenue, rejection rates, submission volumes, and claim lifecycle tracking
- Data export in standard formats for your own records or your accountant
- Help centre access and email support

Delegate accounts are included at no extra cost. You can invite staff members to enter claims or review assessments on your behalf without paying for additional seats.

## How billing works

Meritum processes payments through Stripe. You enter your credit card during onboarding, and Stripe handles all charges, receipts, and payment retries. Meritum does not store your card number directly.

Monthly subscriptions renew on the same calendar day each month. If you signed up on February 15, your next charge is March 15. Annual subscriptions renew on the anniversary of your sign-up date.

If a payment fails, Stripe retries the charge according to its standard retry schedule. During this period your account remains fully active so you do not lose access to your claims or submission history. If all retries fail, your account moves to a suspended state where you can still view and export your data but cannot submit new claims.

## Changing or cancelling your plan

You can switch between monthly and annual billing at any time from your account settings. See [Switching between monthly and annual billing](/help-centre/your-account/switching-between-monthly-and-annual-billing) for step-by-step instructions.

If you decide Meritum is not the right fit, you can cancel from your account settings. Your access continues until the end of your current billing period, and you have a 30-day grace period after cancellation to export your data. See [Cancelling your subscription](/help-centre/your-account/cancelling-your-subscription) for details on how cancellation works and what happens to your data.

## Receipts and tax records

Stripe sends a receipt to your account email address after every successful charge. Each receipt includes the charge amount, the GST portion, and Meritum's GST registration number. These receipts are suitable for claiming business expenses on your tax return.

If you need a receipt re-sent or need to update the email address where receipts are delivered, you can do so from your account settings under billing. You can also view your full payment history in the billing section, which shows every charge, its status, and the associated invoice.
