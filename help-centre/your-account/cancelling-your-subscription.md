You can cancel your Meritum subscription at any time from your account settings. When you cancel, your access continues through the end of your current billing period, followed by a 30-day grace period where you can still view and export your data.

## How to cancel

1. Log in to Meritum and open **Account Settings** from the navigation menu.
2. In the **Billing** section, click **Manage Billing**. This opens the Stripe Customer Portal.
3. In the Customer Portal, select **Cancel plan**.
4. Stripe asks you to confirm. Review the cancellation date shown, then confirm.
5. Close the portal tab. Meritum receives the cancellation automatically via webhook.

You do not need to contact support to cancel. The process is entirely self-serve.

## When cancellation takes effect

For **monthly subscriptions**, cancellation takes effect at the end of your current monthly billing period. If your renewal date is March 15 and you cancel on March 3, you retain full access until March 15.

For **annual subscriptions**, cancellation also takes effect at the end of your current annual billing period. If you paid for a full year on January 10, your access continues until January 10 of the following year. Partial-year refunds are not available for annual plans; you have already paid for the full period and retain access for that entire duration.

## The 30-day grace period

After your billing period ends, your account enters a 30-day grace period. During this window:

- You can log in and view all your claims, patients, assessments, and reports.
- You can export your data in standard formats. See [Exporting your data](/help-centre/your-account/exporting-your-data) for instructions on downloading your records.
- You cannot submit new claims, create patients, or make changes to your account.

Meritum sends you reminder emails at day 7 and day 21 of the grace period so you have time to complete your data export before the window closes.

## What happens to your data after the grace period

Once the 30-day grace period ends, Meritum permanently deletes all Protected Health Information (PHI) associated with your account. This includes patient records, claim data, assessment results, provider profile details, and any uploaded files.

This deletion is irreversible. If you need your data, export it before the grace period ends.

Two categories of records are retained after deletion:

- **Audit logs** are retained for 10 years as required by the Health Information Act (HIA). These logs record actions taken on the platform (such as claim submissions and data access events) but all personally identifiable information is stripped from them before long-term storage.
- **Information Manager Agreement (IMA) records** are also retained for 10 years per HIA requirements. These document your obligations as an information manager under Alberta's health privacy legislation.

## Reactivating after cancellation

If you change your mind during the grace period, you can reactivate your subscription from your account settings. Your data is still intact and your account returns to full functionality once payment resumes.

After the grace period has ended and your data has been deleted, you would need to create a new account and set up your profile, business arrangements, and patient records from scratch.
