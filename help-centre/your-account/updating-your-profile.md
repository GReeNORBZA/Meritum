You can update your professional profile from **Account Settings** at any time after onboarding. Select the section you want to change; each update is saved immediately and audit-logged.

## Changing your specialty or physician type

1. Go to **Account Settings > Professional Profile**.
2. Select a new **specialty** from the dropdown. The list matches the Alberta Health Care Insurance Plan (AHCIP) specialty code table.
3. If needed, change your **physician type** (General Practitioner, Specialist, or Locum).
4. Click **Save**.

A specialty change only affects future claim entry. Historical claims retain the specialty that was active when you submitted them. After the change, the Schedule of Medical Benefits (SOMB) health service code search prioritizes codes relevant to your new specialty, and the rules engine applies the corresponding governing rules going forward. You do not need to re-confirm your business arrangement or complete any additional forms.

Your billing number and College of Physicians and Surgeons of Alberta (CPSA) registration number cannot be changed through the platform. These are tied to your Information Manager Agreement (IMA) and submitter authorization; contact support if either needs correction.

For details on what was entered during onboarding, see [Setting up your professional profile](/help-centre/getting-started/setting-up-your-professional-profile).

## Adding or removing practice locations

1. Go to **Account Settings > Practice Locations**.
2. To add a location, click **Add Location** and fill in the location name, functional centre code, facility number, community code, and address. Click **Save**.
3. To remove a location you no longer use, click **Deactivate** next to it. Deactivated locations no longer appear in the claim entry picker but remain in your location history. Claims previously billed from that location are unaffected.
4. To change your default, click **Set as Default** next to the location where you provide care most often. The default is pre-selected when you create a new claim.

Locum physicians who move between communities can add as many locations as needed. Each location carries its own functional centre and community code, so the correct governing rules and Rural and Remote Northern Program (RRNP) premiums apply automatically when you select that location on a claim.

## Updating your business arrangements

1. Go to **Account Settings > Business Arrangements**.
2. To add a new Business Arrangement (BA), click **Add BA** and enter the BA number. If you are adding a Primary Care Panel and Continuity Model (PCPCM) BA alongside an existing fee-for-service BA, the platform launches the dual-BA setup flow so both arrangements are configured correctly for claim routing.
3. To deactivate a BA you no longer use, click **Deactivate**. You must keep at least one active BA to submit claim batches.

Each new BA requires a separate AHC11236 Submitter Authorization form. The platform generates the pre-filled form when you add the BA; download it, sign it, and submit it to Alberta Health. The BA starts in **Pending** status and moves to **Active** once Alberta Health processes the form.

For background on BA numbers and the AHC11236 process, see [Adding your business arrangement numbers](/help-centre/getting-started/adding-your-business-arrangement-numbers).

## Changing your WCB configuration

1. Go to **Account Settings > WCB Billing**.
2. To add a new Workers' Compensation Board (WCB) contract, click **Add Configuration** and enter your Contract ID, Role code, and Skill code. The platform calculates which WCB form types that combination permits.
3. To remove a contract you no longer hold, click **Deactivate** next to it.

If you hold multiple WCB contracts (for example, a general practice contract and a separate Occupational Injury Service contract), each one appears as a separate configuration with its own permitted form types. When you create a WCB claim, you select which configuration to use.
